# USC CPS-VIDA Lab Website Redesign

## Lab name
CPS-VIDA Lab

## Current public tagline
Verification, Intelligence, Design, and Analysis

## Initial research scope
The lab works at the intersection of:
- Formal methods
- Control theory
- Cyber-physical systems
- Artificial intelligence

The current public site identifies these directions:
- Verification of autonomous cyber-physical systems
- Specification languages for cyber-physical systems
- Monitoring security of cyber-physical systems
- Verifiable and safe controller synthesis for autonomy
- Probabilistic reasoning about time-series data using temporal logic

## Desired information architecture
### Home
- Clear research identity
- Short mission statement
- Research areas
- Selected publications
- News
- Link to people and contact pages

### Research
- Research-area overview
- Four to five research themes
- Space for future project highlights

### People
- Faculty director
- PhD students
- Visiting scholars
- Alumni
- Every person must come from editable local content files

### Publications
- Grouped by year
- Each entry should support Paper, Code, Project, Video, and DOI links when available
- Do not include fabricated publication metadata

### Contact
- USC affiliation area
- Contact placeholder
- Optional recruiting/joining section placeholder

## Content status
The current public site is only a starting point. Its people, titles, and publication information must be confirmed before final delivery.

## Visual direction
The website should feel technically rigorous, modern, and academic. It should not resemble a startup landing page or copy Stanford IRIS branding.
